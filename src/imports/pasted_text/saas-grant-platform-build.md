Complete the SaaS application build as an AI-powered Grant Operations Platform, not just a grant tracker.

The product should help organizations discover funding, write proposals, manage awards, track partners, monitor performance metrics, generate reports, and stay compliant across the full grant lifecycle. It should support multiple organizations/workspaces, including separate entities such as TwiddleU and Jobvair, while allowing users to manage funding opportunities for each organization independently.

Keep the current visual style, sidebar, colors, dashboard cards, charts, AI assistant, discovery page, recommendations page, and pipeline page. Expand all pending pages into polished, production-ready SaaS screens with realistic content, tables, filters, modals, empty states, loading states, and AI-powered action buttons.

Build out these modules:

1. Organization Intelligence
Create a full Organization Profile page where users can store mission, vision, programs, service areas, target populations, geographic focus, certifications, EIN, UEI, budgets, board members, staff bios, prior awards, partners, uploaded documents, impact metrics, and strategic priorities. Include an “AI Readiness Score” and “Missing Information” checklist.

2. Grant Discovery
Enhance Grant Discovery so AI can search and recommend grants based on each organization’s mission, products, programs, documents, geography, partners, funding history, and capacity. Each grant card should show match score, eligibility fit, geographic fit, budget fit, deadline, estimated effort, win probability, and AI recommendation.

3. Proposal Builder
Build a full Proposal Builder page. Include sections for executive summary, needs statement, project design, work plan, evaluation plan, sustainability, budget narrative, attachments, and compliance checklist. Add AI actions such as “Draft Section,” “Improve Response,” “Check Against Rubric,” “Generate Budget Narrative,” and “Create Letter of Support.”

4. Budget Builder
Create a Budget Builder page with federal and foundation budget templates. Include personnel, fringe, travel, equipment, supplies, contractual, construction, other, indirect, match, program income, and year-by-year budget tables. Add AI buttons to generate ED-524, SF-424A, budget narratives, and Excel exports.

5. Partner CRM
Create a Partners page to track universities, employers, workforce agencies, evaluators, subcontractors, fiscal sponsors, community organizations, and funders. Include contacts, role, relationship stage, associated grants, letters of support, MOUs, meeting notes, tasks, and AI-suggested partner opportunities.

6. Document Library
Create a Documents page where users can upload and organize IRS letters, 990s, audits, budgets, strategic plans, resumes, letters of support, MOUs, past proposals, board rosters, photos, logos, and reports. Add AI document intelligence that extracts key facts and makes documents searchable.

7. Award Management
Add an Award Management module for grants that have been awarded. Track award amount, budget period, reporting deadlines, deliverables, performance measures, approved budget categories, match requirements, reimbursement status, invoices, and compliance tasks.

8. Metrics & Impact Dashboard
Create a Reporting page that tracks outputs and outcomes by grant and organization. Include participants served, demographics, services delivered, employment outcomes, training completions, placements, retention, wages, partner engagement, budget spent, match documented, and progress toward goals. Add export options for board reports, annual reports, and grant reports.

9. AI Reporting Engine
Add functionality for generating quarterly reports, annual reports, funder updates, board reports, and impact summaries using grant data, budgets, documents, and metrics. Include an AI-generated narrative preview and editable sections.

10. Compliance & Alerts
Create compliance features across the platform. Show alerts for upcoming deadlines, missing attachments, late reports, budget variance, missing match documentation, incomplete metrics, required approvals, and funder-specific reporting requirements.

11. Calendar
Build a calendar page showing application deadlines, reporting deadlines, renewal dates, task deadlines, award dates, meetings, and internal review dates. Include filters by organization, grant, status, and owner.

12. Settings
Build Settings with workspace management, user roles, permissions, billing, integrations, notification preferences, AI settings, data privacy, and organization switching.

13. AI Assistant
Expand the AI Assistant so it can answer questions such as:
- What grants should TwiddleU apply for this month?
- Which grants fit Jobvair?
- What funding opportunities support women’s economic advancement?
- Draft a proposal for this grant.
- Generate a four-year budget.
- What reports are due this quarter?
- Are we missing any compliance items?
- Which grants are most worth our time?

The AI should feel deeply aware of each organization’s profile, documents, partners, prior proposals, budgets, and metrics.

Design requirements:
- Premium modern SaaS interface
- Clean white workspace with soft teal/blue/green accents
- Rounded cards, clear hierarchy, and polished dashboards
- Tables with filters, sorting, tags, and inline status badges
- Kanban views, calendar views, and detailed record pages
- Interactive modals for adding grants, partners, documents, metrics, and tasks
- AI buttons throughout the product
- Responsive layout for desktop and tablet
- Accessibility-conscious color contrast and readable typography
- Realistic demo data for at least two organizations: TwiddleU and Jobvair

The final product should feel like a combination of Salesforce, Notion, Linear, Instrumentl, ChatGPT Teams, and Monday.com, but purpose-built for grant-funded and mission-driven organizations.

Do not leave any sidebar pages as pending placeholders. Build every module into a polished, clickable prototype with realistic workflows.
